FAQ

Why does the assessment come first?

The assessment keeps the subscription focused on work that is actually worth doing. Before we start building automations or AI systems, I look at your current tools, repeated tasks, handoffs, and bottlenecks so we can identify the highest-leverage opportunities first.

What happens during the assessment?

We'll review where your team is spending unnecessary time, where work is being copied between tools, where follow-up or handoffs break down, and where AI or automation could realistically help. Afterward, you get a written roadmap with prioritized opportunities and recommended next steps.

What happens after the assessment?

You'll leave with a roadmap and recommendations you can use on your own. From there, you can continue into the subscription and work with me directly to implement the roadmap, handle the technical setup, improve the systems over time, and take on new requests as they come up.

Can I send as many requests as I want?

Yes. Once you're in the subscription, you can send as many requests as you like. I work through them one at a time so the work stays focused, finished, and useful.

What counts as a request?

A request is a specific improvement you want handled, such as automating lead intake, drafting customer support replies from inbox scans, building an internal dashboard, setting up an AI assistant, cleaning up a handoff, or connecting tools your team already uses. The possibilities are endless... try me.

How fast are requests completed?

Small requests may move quickly. Larger requests are broken into smaller pieces, with progress updates every 24-48 hours while active. The goal is steady visible progress, not disappearing for weeks and returning with a giant reveal.

Do you help keep systems updated over time?

Yes. AI tools, automation platforms, and software features are changing quickly. Part of the ongoing support is keeping your systems current, improving performance, replacing outdated pieces when better options emerge, and making sure what we build keeps working as your tools evolve.

What kinds of requests are too big for the subscription?

Full SaaS products, major website rebuilds, complex migrations, emergency production support, or broad technical leadership usually sit outside the subscription. If something is too large, I'll tell you and we can decide whether it should become a separate project.

Do I need to be technical?

No. You bring the business context: what is slow, repetitive, messy, or expensive. I translate that into tools, automations, prompts, docs, workflows, and Loom walkthroughs your team can actually use.
