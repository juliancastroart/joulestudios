This is a working session, not a lecture.

Most lawyers are either not using AI yet, or they are stuck using it only as a chat function. The next step is turning Claude into a repeatable workflow the firm can use: intake follow-up, drafting support, client communication, firm checklists, internal SOPs, or the first version of a Claude Skill your team can reuse.

The goal is simple: use the prep call to pick the right target, use the live session to build the first useful version, and use the follow-up call to tighten it after you use it in the firm.

  • Automate intake and follow-up: qualify new leads, summarize consult notes, draft next-step emails, and prepare handoff notes so no inquiry sits untouched.
  • Save time on admin work: turn repeated status updates, scheduling replies, document requests, and internal questions into workflows your team can run without waiting on you.
  • Improve repeated drafting work: build Claude instructions for common petitions, demand-letter sections, client update drafts, discovery summaries, or first-pass document outlines while keeping attorney review in place.
  • Build firm knowledge into Claude: capture your intake criteria, matter checklists, tone rules, SOPs, and client communication standards so your team stops asking the same questions every week.

Frequently asked questions

A few practical details about the workshop.

Will this work for my practice area?

Yes. The workshop is built around your workflow, not your practice area. PI, family, immigration, criminal defense, estate, business: the underlying mechanics are the same. You have intake to process, documents to draft, clients to update, and internal questions your team asks you on repeat. Claude Skills can help with all of it.

In the prep call, we pick one workflow specific to your firm and build around that. So whether you are qualifying car accident leads, drafting petitions, or generating client status updates, you leave with something built for your practice, not a generic template.

What will I actually leave with?

Three things, all usable Friday morning:

  1. A working Claude Skill, configured for your firm. Not a demo, not a template. A tool that runs against your actual workflow. We pick the workflow in the prep call and build it together in the room.
  2. The framework to build more. Once you have built one Skill, you can build a Skill for intake, drafting, follow-ups, internal SOPs, client communication, or another repeated workflow. You leave knowing how the pieces fit together, not just having watched.
  3. Documentation you can hand to your team. A short written guide for the workflow we built, so the people in your firm who did not attend can use it without you re-explaining it.

The follow-up call two weeks later is to make sure all three are actually working in your firm after the workshop.

Why should I trust you with my firm's workflows?

Fair question. I am not a lawyer and I have not run a law firm.

What I have done is spend the last couple of years building AI systems that run inside service businesses: internal tools, automations, Claude workflows, and processes that take repetitive work off operators' plates. Most recently, I helped clients cut a recurring analysis process from four hours to about thirty minutes by building the right Skill around it, and I have automated customer support emails to save hours of work per week. That is the same work we will do in the room, applied to your firm.

You are also protected operationally. Nothing leaves your Claude account. We work entirely within your own login, so your firm's data stays where it should.

If you want to talk before reserving, email me at julian@joulestudios.com and we will get on a 15-minute call.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. This workshop is for lawyers, owners, and operators, not developers. If you can explain how your firm works, we can turn that knowledge into useful Claude instructions and workflows.

Do I need a Claude account?

Yes. Please create a Claude account before the event and subscribe to Claude Pro or higher. Bring your login and laptop so we can build in your own account. After you reserve your seat, I will send a short setup note so you arrive ready to build.

What if I already use ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, or another AI tool?

Great. Bring your best prompts, documents, and workflows. We will help you move the useful parts into Claude and shift from chatting with AI to having AI complete specific tasks for your firm.

Do I need to bring a laptop?

Yes. This is hands-on. Bring a laptop you can use comfortably for the full session.

What about client confidentiality?

Do not bring privileged or confidential client materials into the room. We can work from sample, anonymized, or redacted workflows. This event is educational and operational, not legal advice.

Can I send someone from my firm?

Yes, if they understand your firm's operations and can help implement the workflow afterward. Each attendee needs their own seat.

What happens after I reserve interest?
